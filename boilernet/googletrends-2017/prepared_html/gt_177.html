Why did Cardi B and Nicki Minaj fight at New York Fashion Week and what was said about baby Kulture?

Tobi Akingbade Saturday 8 Sep 2018 12:39 pm
Share this article via facebook Share this article via twitter Share this article via messenger
Share this with
Share this article via email Share this article via flipboard Copy link

Nicki Minaj has spent the last month sparring with her celeb peers, but now things have taken a seriously bitter turn as Cardi B ‘hurled her shoe’ at the rapper during a New York Fashion Week Party last night.

The rappers, both from the city, were pitted against each other early on this year by fans who drew similarities between the two as Cardi B rose to fame.

They appeared to make up at the Met Gala in May after rumours emerged of a feud when Cardi and Minaj both submitted verses to Migos’ single Motorsport.

Cardi, whose husband Offset is in Migos, apparently reacted in a negative way to Minaj’s verse, was offended by the comments.

When the pair made up, both seemed confused with the details of their squabble, making it known as the rap beef that never really was.

Or so we thought. Now things have escalated dramatically after Nicki reportedly made a comment about Cardi’s ability to raise her newborn Kutlure.

So what exactly did Nicki say? And what led up to that moment where Cardi left the NY party with her dressed ripper and a bruise on her face.

We have given you a brief history.

Where it all began

In March 2017, Nicki seemingly liked a comment taking digs on a video of the 25-year-old rapping. The comment read: ‘Dumb ass bars,’ but many fans pointed out that the social media activity could be fake.

But the tension built up from there.

In August that same year, Nicki featured on No Flags with 21 Savage and Offset.

She rapped: ‘I heard these labels are trying to make another me / Everything you’re getting little hoe is because of me.’

Many believed it was about Cardi, but Nicki later tweeted a response to backlash saying that the line was not even about her.

Did fans create Cardi B vs Nicki Minaj?

Fans continued to pit the women against each other in online debates about who is the best woman rapper in the industry.

Later that month, Cardi called out a mystery woman during a performance in August at the height of her positive feedback for her song Bodak Yellow.

She said on OMA PS1 stage: ‘You know this bitch she never fucking liked me, and all of a sudden she want to be friends with me — no, bitch …

Advertisement
Advertisement

‘I still don’t like you bitch.’

Fans speculated that she was directing the diss at Nicki.

Cardi declares she wants no war

However, at the 2017 MTV VMA’s awards on 28 August, 2017, Cardi insisted that she wanted nothing to do with an ‘industry beef’.

She said: ‘I don’t want problems with anybody. I just want to make music and make money.

‘I don’t have time to look at other women and what they’re doing. I don’t have to do that whole industry beef, you know? I get it poppin’ with these hands.’

Back to beefin’

Later that summer Cardi jumped on G-Eazy’s song No Limit and took aim at unnamed haters, as she spat: ‘My career takin’ off / These hoes jogging in place / Swear these hoes run they mouth / How these hoes out of shape? / Can you stop with all the subs? / Bitch I ain’t Jared.’

Again, listeners were pretty convinced it was about Nicki forcing Cardi to clarify in an interview with The Breakfast club that she was taking aim at haters from her home in New York – and not Nicki (who is also from the city but whatever).

Advertisement

So far the shots fired, and digs being taken were pretty ambivalent but when both rappers appeared on Migos’ track Motorsport thinks got a bit personal.

Motorport is where the tensions get real

The song and music video was released in November and December, respectively, but was clouded with a public feud between the two over changed lyrics.

When MotorSport first came out, people initially thought the two were taking shots at each other on the song – but there was some confusion with last minute alterations.

Cardi get cagey and Nicki cries

In an interview with Capital Xtra, Cardi became unusually cagey when she asked about the process of making the song.

‘When I heard the track, her verse wasn’t finished. It was not the verse that is on right now,’ Cardi revealed. ‘Quavo told me to get on the song, and I just felt it was a perfect opportunity for me to be on a track that’s big like them.’

Nicki responded on Twitter to her 21.3million followers, she wrote: ‘How can you say someone changed their verse & forget to say Quavo TOLD me to remove my singing part (which I loved) & Atlantic told me to remove your name from my verse per your request?

‘So how were those changes gonna happen if I didn’t “change” my verse? #NickiDay #ChunLi.’

Advertisement

Nicki’s original verse referenced Cardi – as well as football coach Vince Lombardi – and she appeared to shout her fellow rapper out.

‘If Cardi the QB, I’m Nick Lombardi,’ she previously rapped – before it was changed to ‘If Quavo the QB, I’m Nick Lombardi.’

Weeks later Cardi told Beast 1 Radio that their tension was fan-made and days later Nicki was moved to tears during an interview when she was asked about the Invasion of Privacy rapper.

In the interview with Zane Lowe on Beats , Nicki said: ‘The only thing with Cardi that really, really, really hurt my feelings was the first interview she did after Motorsport came out.’

She added: ‘The first interview she did after Motorsport came out, it just really hurt me. She looked so aggravated and angry and the only thing she said was, “Oh, I didn’t hear that. I didn’t hear that verse.” I was like, what?’

A truce

However, in May, the two set aside any tension in a sweet moment on the Met Gala red carpet, with Cardi later admitting that the ‘misunderstanding’ upset her.

At the time she was seven months pregnant with Kulture and decided to clear up some of the rumours during an interview with legendary talk show host Howard Stern at the SiriusXM Studios.

‘I never was feuding with anybody; there was a misunderstanding,’ Cardi said. ‘I think she felt a certain type of way about something. I definitely felt a certain type of way about something.’

She added: ‘My feelings was really hurt.’

The fight

Ever since then things have been pretty quiet on the Nicki vs Cardi font – apart from when fans speculated Cardi took a dig at Nicki again at the 2018 VMAs.

Now last night things took a turn for the worst at the Harper’s Bazaar bash when the Be Careful songstress approached her sparring partner’s table over remarks made about her mothering, and, by all accounts, it immediately kicked off.

According to TMZ, Cardi darted straight for Nicki and belted: ‘Let me tell you something…’

Though, her reps have since insisted that the mother-of-one one was not looking for a fight, she simply wanted to address comments made about her abilities as a mother, two months after giving birth to baby Kulture.

Cardi addressed the situation, which left her with a bruise and ripped dress, on her Instagram page claiming that Nicki ‘mentioned her child’ and made comments about her abilities to look after her daughter.

Metro.co.uk has contacted reps for both Nicki Minaj and Cardi B for comment.
